Team Comp Strategy Guide

168 champions · conceptual reference · current as of Season 2025

A team composition isn't just five picks — it's a strategic identity. Every comp has a win condition, a power window, and a set of things it wants to do before or after the enemy. Understanding those three things lets you make better picks, read matchups, and adapt mid-game.

The three questions every comp must answer

How do we start fights?

Engage, pick, dive, or poke the enemy into a bad fight. Teams with no initiation tool must rely on the enemy making a mistake.

When is our window?

Every comp has a phase — early, mid, or late — where it peaks. Play around that window and close out the enemy's.

What do we target?

Carries, tanks, or objectives. Some comps blow up one target; others apply AoE pressure. Know which, and position to execute it.

Range profile matters

Melee-heavy comps

More melee = harder to kite. Strong in poke matchups if they can close the gap with engage. Vulnerable to long-range poke and disengage. Require good engage tools or waveclear to survive the poke phase.

Ranged-heavy comps

More range = better poke and disengage. Thrive against frontline-heavy comps that can't close distance. Vulnerable to hard engage and assassins who bypass the frontline entirely.

Internal synergy types
Damage + access
A tank or diver creates the opening; the carry executes. Classic: Malphite ult into ADC cleanup.
Poke + siege

CC chains
Stacking crowd control locks a target long enough to delete them regardless of damage type.
Protect the carry
Stack peel around a hypercarry. Win condition: reach late game with carry alive and full items.
Reset chains
Champions who reset on kills (Katarina, Jinx passive) snowball cleans into unstoppable momentum.
Deny their win-con
Counterpick mechanics: Vex vs. dashes, Rammus vs. AD, Poppy vs. engage, Mordekaiser vs. split.

Comp archetypes describe how a team wants to win a fight. A comp can blend two archetypes (poke-engage, dive-split) but should have a clear primary identity to avoid confusion about win conditions.

Riot's class system organizes champions by how they fight. Understanding each class's purpose helps you build comps that are internally consistent — you know what every player is supposed to do when a fight starts.

Power timing is the most misunderstood concept in solo queue. Every champion has a phase window. Play inside your window and you win. Play outside it and you bleed.

Key principles
If your window is earlier
Force fights. Deny farm. Take every objective. Do not let the game go late — you are not the same champion at 40 minutes as at 20.
If your window is later
Survive. Farm. Identify which laners can hold a lead long enough for you to come online. Do not take risky fights for no reason.
Window clash
When a mid-spiker meets a late-scaler, the mid-spiker must snowball or die. If the game hits 35 minutes, the late-scaler wins by default.
Team timing mismatch
A mix of early and late peaks is dangerous — your early players don't have help when they need it. Align timing within 1–2 tiers.
Objective tempo
Power windows don't run on clocks — they run on objectives. Rift Herald, dragon, Baron, and towers are the checkpoints where you spend your power or lose it.
Hybrid is a draft decision
Hybrid champions (Varus, Kayn) shift identity based on build. Know which identity you're committing to before the game starts.

Matchup logic is about understanding what the enemy comp needs to function — then taking it away. Every strategy has a structural weakness. The goal is to identify it in draft, not at 25 minutes.

What each archetype needs — and how to deny it
Item-based counters (Season 2025)

Grievous Wounds

Reduces healing by 40–60%. Hard counter to Soraka, Vladimir, Yuumi, Dr. Mundo, Warwick, Aatrox. If their comp has 2+ major healers, someone on your team must build GW before third item.

Armor / Magic pen

Void Staff (magic pen) and Lord Dominik's/Last Whisper (armor pen). If their frontline is Ornn, Malphite, and Leona, armor pen matters more than raw attack speed on your AD carry.

Zhonya's Hourglass / Stopwatch

Stasis survives burst combos and forces the assassin to leave without a kill. Essential for immobile mages and targeted carries facing Zed, Talon, or Nocturne.

Quicksilver Sash (QSS) → Mercurial Scimitar

QSS removes most CC including suppression (Malzahar, Warwick). Upgrades to Mercurial Scimitar for crit-building ADCs. Note: Silvermere Dawn was removed from the game in Season 2025. No longer counters Mordekaiser's Realm of Death.

Cleanse (summoner spell)

Removes most CC plus summoner spell debuffs (Exhaust, Ignite). Unlike QSS, it does not remove suppression. Best on carries expecting heavy CC lockdown who don't build crit (can't use Mercurial).

Banshee's Veil

Spell shield blocks the first ability hit. Crucial against point-and-click engage (Blitzcrank hook, Fiddle fear). Can negate an entire pick comp's initiation if timed correctly.
